Icon Concepts

Five directions for favicon, Apple Touch, Open Graph, and Twitter Card

Concept 1

Split Dollar

A circle split down the center — war red on the left, care teal on the right — with a bold dollar sign straddling both halves. The core tension of the site in one glyph: the same money, two possible futures.

+ Instantly communicates "money split two ways"
+ Reads clearly at every size — the $ is unmistakable at 16px
+ Uses the site's exact color palette
– Doesn't reference the three sectors specifically
– Dollar-sign icons are common in finance/fintech
Concept 2

Missile to House

A red missile in the upper-left breaking apart into a teal house in the lower-right. A visual metaphor for transformation — destroying abroad vs. building at home. The jagged break represents the choice point.

+ Most literal representation of the site's argument
+ Emotionally striking — war weapon becoming a home
+ Unique — not a common icon pattern
– Detail gets lost at 16–32px favicon size
– More illustrative than iconic — harder to recognize as a brand mark
Concept 3

Three Pillars

Three ascending bars in the site's sector colors — teal (health), blue (education), gold (infrastructure) — representing what we could build instead. Simple, data-forward, and directly maps to the navbar's three sections.

+ Reads perfectly at every size — even 16px
+ Color-coded to match the site's three sections
+ Signals "data analysis" which matches the site's tone
– Could be mistaken for a generic analytics/dashboard icon
– Less emotionally evocative than the other concepts
Concept 4

The Toggle

A UI toggle switch — flipped from the red X (war) to the teal checkmark (home). Three colored dots underneath represent the sectors. The message: this is a choice we can make.

+ Conceptually powerful — "we could flip the switch"
+ Modern, UI-native metaphor that feels interactive
+ Clean silhouette at small sizes
– The X and dots disappear at favicon size — only the toggle reads
– Might feel more "tech product" than "public policy analysis"
Concept 5

Home Chart

A house outline containing three ascending bars in the sector colors — health, education, infrastructure rising inside the home. It directly ties "home" (the hero tagline) to the data (the site's substance). A small red X in the corner nods to the cost being rejected.

+ Directly echoes the headline "The Cost of Home"
+ Combines the emotional (house) with the analytical (chart bars)
+ Three colors map perfectly to the navbar and section structure
+ House silhouette reads well even at 16px
– Slightly more complex than a pure geometric mark
– The red X is too subtle at small sizes (but removable)

Recommendation: Concept 5 (Home Chart) is the strongest overall. It's the only icon that simultaneously communicates the headline ("home"), the site structure (three sectors), and the analytical tone (chart bars) — all in a shape that holds up from 1200px OG cards down to a 16px favicon tab. Concept 3 (Three Pillars) is the safest fallback if you want maximum simplicity.

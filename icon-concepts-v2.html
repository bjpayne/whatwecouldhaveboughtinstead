Split Circle — Five Variations

All based on Concept 1's split-circle form with Concept 2's figurative icons replacing the dollar sign. War on the left (red), Home on the right (teal).

Variation 1

Missile vs. House

The most literal pairing. A sleek missile silhouette on the red half, a simple house with door and windows on the teal half. The two shapes are roughly the same visual weight, creating a balanced composition. The missile's pointed nose and the house's peaked roof create a subtle mirror symmetry.

+ Immediately readable — no interpretation needed
+ The missile and house silhouettes both hold at small sizes
+ Directly references "The Cost of Home" tagline
– The house only represents one of three sectors
– Could feel heavy-handed in its literalness
Variation 2

Crosshair vs. Heart

A weapon's targeting reticle on the red half, a heart on the teal half. More abstract than Variation 1 — this is about intent rather than objects. The crosshair says "destruction by design," the heart says "care by choice." Both shapes are circular, creating visual harmony within the split circle.

+ Emotionally resonant — targeting vs. caring
+ Both shapes are inherently circular, fitting the container perfectly
+ Heart is universal, not limited to just healthcare
– Crosshair detail (tick marks) gets lost at 16px
– Heart could read as "love" or "charity" rather than "domestic investment"
Variation 3

Explosion vs. Seedling

A starburst explosion on the red half, a growing seedling on the teal half. This is the most metaphorical pairing — destruction vs. growth. The explosion is chaotic and angular, the seedling is organic and upward-reaching. The contrast in shape language is as powerful as the color contrast.

+ Strongest conceptual contrast — chaos vs. growth
+ The seedling implies investment in the future, spanning all sectors
+ Both shapes are distinctive — no confusion with other brands
– Starburst could be read as a "new" or "sale" badge, not an explosion
– The seedling is delicate and thins out at small sizes
Variation 4

Bomb vs. Three Sectors

A falling bomb on the red half. On the teal half, a vertical stack of three icons representing each sector: a medical cross (health), an open book (education), and a wrench (infrastructure). This variation directly maps to the site's three-section structure.

+ Only version that explicitly references all three sectors
+ The bomb is a bold, unmistakable war symbol
+ Creates visual "inventory" feel — what we could have instead
– Three small icons on the right side become unreadable at 32px
– Feels busier and less elegant than single-icon pairings
– The wrench/book/cross read as generic clip art at distance
Variation 5

Down vs. Up

The most minimal and abstract variation. A bold downward arrow on red (decline, destruction, money falling away), a bold upward arrow on teal (growth, building, investment rising). Pure geometry — no figurative imagery at all. Three small dots crown the upward arrow as a subtle nod to the three sectors.

+ Maximum legibility at every size — even 12px
+ The cleanest, most "logo-grade" version
+ Universal language — no cultural interpretation needed
+ Works in any context: social card, tab, watermark
– So abstract it could mean anything (stocks, analytics, etc.)
– Doesn't reference "home," war, or any specific sector

Recommendation: Variation 1 (Missile vs. House) is the strongest pick. It's the only version where both halves are instantly identifiable at every size — a pointed missile and a peaked house are among the most universally recognized silhouettes in existence. The shapes mirror each other (pointed tops, wider bases), creating visual balance. And the house directly echoes your "Cost of Home" headline. Variation 5 (Down vs. Up) is the runner-up if you want maximum simplicity and cross-platform scalability — it's the safest favicon and will never lose legibility.
